Who I Am

I enjoy designing systems that feel fair but challenging. When I build a game, I start from core mechanics: how it moves, how enemies react, and how players read danger. Once the logic feels solid, I layer in visuals, sound, and atmosphere to finish the experience.

My work often combines technical problem-solving with visual design. I use code to drive behaviours like collision, state machines, and field-of-view detection, while also paying attention to layout, typography, and how information is presented to the player.

How I Work

Experiment First

I prototype quickly using simple visuals, then focus on how it feels to move, jump, dodge, and react before further polishing.

Player-Centered

I think about how players will learn the game: what they see first, what might be their next move, and how they understand feedback like damage, stun, or danger.

Always Iterating

I refine by testing, breaking things, and adjusting codes. Small tweaks to speed, timing, or hitboxes can completely change the quality of the game.
